5 AI Prompts Every Technical Recruiter Should Be Using

Copy these into Claude (claude.ai), fill in the [BRACKETS], and watch them work. These are pulled directly from the 50+ prompt library inside The AI Recruiter OS.

1. Boolean Search String Generator

Sourcing • Saves 30+ minutes per search
I'm sourcing for a [ROLE TITLE] at a [COMPANY TYPE — e.g., Series B fintech / enterprise SaaS / AI startup].

Key requirements:
- [REQUIREMENT 1]
- [REQUIREMENT 2]
- [REQUIREMENT 3]

Generate 5 Boolean search strings I can use on:
1. LinkedIn Recruiter
2. Google (X-ray search for LinkedIn profiles)
3. GitHub

For each, optimize for precision over recall — I'd rather see 20 highly relevant results than 200 loosely related ones. Include exclusion terms to filter out irrelevant profiles.
Pro tip: Run the top-performing Boolean string, then paste 3-5 result profiles back into Claude and ask it to refine the search based on what's working.

2. Screening Question Generator

Candidate Evaluation • Better screens in 2 minutes
Generate screening questions for a [ROLE TITLE] candidate. The role requires:
- [KEY REQUIREMENT 1]
- [KEY REQUIREMENT 2]
- [KEY REQUIREMENT 3]

Create 3 categories of questions:
1. Technical depth (5 questions) — assess hands-on expertise, not textbook knowledge
2. Problem-solving and judgment (3 questions) — scenarios that reveal how they think
3. Motivation and fit (3 questions) — understand why they want THIS role

For each question, include:
- The question itself
- What a strong answer sounds like
- What a red-flag answer sounds like
- A follow-up probe to go deeper
Pro tip: Customize the role requirements for each search. The more specific you are about the tech stack and team context, the better the questions.

3. Outreach Personalizer

Outreach • 2x your response rate
Personalize this outreach template for the candidate below:

Template:
[PASTE YOUR TEMPLATE]

Candidate details:
- Name: [NAME]
- Current role: [TITLE at COMPANY]
- Notable experience: [KEY DETAILS FROM LINKEDIN]
- Something unique: [BLOG POST / OSS PROJECT / CONFERENCE TALK]

Role I'm pitching: [ROLE TITLE] at [COMPANY]

Write 3 versions:
1. Direct and professional
2. Casual and conversational
3. Leading with a specific compliment about their work

Keep each under 300 characters for LinkedIn. Make each feel genuinely personal, not templated.
Pro tip: The version that references something specific about their work (option 3) almost always outperforms generic messages.

4. Weekly HM Update Drafter

HM Management • 5-minute weekly updates
Generate a weekly recruiting update email for [HM NAME] on the [ROLE TITLE] search.

This week's data:
- New candidates sourced: [#]
- Outreach sent: [#]
- Response rate: [%]
- Screens completed: [#]
- Candidates submitted: [NAMES + BRIEF NOTES]
- Interviews scheduled: [NAMES + DATES]
- Blockers: [ANY]

Format as a brief, scannable email with bullet points. Include a "Next Week" section with planned actions. End with any decisions or input needed from the HM.
Pro tip: Send this every Friday at the same time. Consistency builds trust with hiring managers faster than anything else.

5. Counter-Offer Response Script

Offer & Close • Save deals that are slipping
My candidate just received a counter-offer from their current employer.

- Our offer: [DETAILS]
- Their counter-offer: [DETAILS]
- Candidate's original reasons for leaving: [REASONS]
- Their current emotional state: [e.g., conflicted, leaning toward staying]

Help me:
1. Frame the conversation — what to say in the first 2 minutes
2. Remind them of their original motivations without being manipulative
3. Address the counter-offer point by point
4. Share the data on counter-offer outcomes (most leave within 6-12 months)
5. Give them a framework for making the decision themselves
6. Know when to back off — what signs tell me the deal is lost?

Tone: empathetic advisor, not salesperson.
Pro tip: This prompt has saved multiple deals. The key is using it BEFORE the call, not during. Prepare your talking points, then have a real conversation.
